Portugal

I work on the Portuguese economy and its business cycle — through the Portuguese Business Cycle Dating Committee at the Fundação Francisco Manuel dos Santos (FFMS), and through occasional public-facing work.

Business Cycle Dating Committee

I am part of the Portuguese Business Cycle Dating Committee — an independent committee of experts chaired by Ricardo Reis (LSE) and funded by FFMS — that dates the peaks and troughs of the Portuguese business cycle, the Portuguese counterpart to the NBER and CEPR dating committees.

The committee has produced over a century of business-cycle analysis (1910–present), collected in the book Crises in the Portuguese Economy. My contributions include maintaining the underlying database of 500+ macroeconomic indicators, the time-series analysis behind the dating decisions, and co-authoring the accompanying reports.
