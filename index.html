Hi there!

I'm Alberto Ferreira.

PhD Researcher in Economics · European University Institute, Florence

I'm an economist working on firm dynamics and macroeconomics. I ask how firms reallocate resources — over time and across the cross-section — and what that means for aggregates like business dynamism and productivity. My approach is micro-to-macro: I bring census-level firm data into models with real and financial frictions.

About

Before Florence, I read economics at Nova SBE in Lisbon and took graduate degrees in economics and mathematics through the LSE and the University of London. Along the way I spent time at the European Central Bank, working with medium-scale DSGE models in the Financial Research Division.

I'm a fifth-year PhD candidate at the EUI, where my supervisors are Russell Cooper and Giancarlo Corsetti. My current projects look at how firms grow and finance themselves in an increasingly intangible economy, whether the usual AR(1) assumption on productivity is harmless, and why investment dynamism has declined across countries since the financial crisis. You can read more on the research page.

Outside my own research, I consult for CompNet at the Halle Institute for Economic Research (IWH) and advise the Portuguese Business Cycle Dating Committee.
